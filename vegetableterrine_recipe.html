Nine Vegetable Terrine


Description

A common French dish utilizes nine different types of vegetables set in a green gelatinous compound. This dish is intended to savor the harmony created by the sweetness of each vegetable according to chef Kojiro Shinomiya.


Ingredients

  • Cabbage
  • Carrot
  • Okra
  • Zucchini
  • Cauliflower
  • Yellow summer squash
  • White and green asparagus
  • Baby corn
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to the boil. Salt it and blanch the beet greens for 1 minute. Remove the leaves and immediately rinse under ice-cold water to set their color.
  2. Line a buttered terrine mold with a piece of parchment. Neatly lay in the beet leaves to cover the bottom and sides completely.
  3. Cook the cauliflower, carrots and peas one at a time in the same pot of boiling salted water, until very tender. Remove them and immediately rinse in ice-cold water to preserve their color. Drain well. Roast the pepper until very soft. Peel, seed and cut into pieces.
